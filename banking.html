Common Scenarios

How to Report Fraud

  1. Contact your financial institution immediately

    Call the customer service number on the back of your card or on your statement.

  2. Place a fraud alert on your credit reports

    Contact one of the three major credit bureaus (Equifax, Experian, or TransUnion) to place a fraud alert.

  3. File a report with the FTC

    Visit reportfraud.ftc.gov to file a report.

  4. File a police report

    Contact your local police department to file a report, especially for significant financial losses.

  5. Document everything

    Keep records of all communications, including dates, names, and what was discussed.

Lost or Stolen Cards

  1. Contact your card issuer immediately

    Call the customer service number on your statement or the emergency number provided by your bank.

  2. Review recent transactions

    Check your account for any unauthorized transactions and report them to your bank.

  3. Request a replacement card

    Ask your bank to issue a new card with a new number.

  4. Update automatic payments

    Once you receive your new card, update any automatic payments linked to your old card.

  5. Monitor your account

    Continue to monitor your account for any suspicious activity.

Report Phishing

  1. Don't click on suspicious links or attachments

    Never click on links or download attachments from suspicious emails or texts.

  2. Forward phishing emails

    Forward suspicious emails to phishing@irs.gov (for IRS-related) or reportphishing@apwg.org (general).

  3. Report to your financial institution

    Forward the suspicious email to your bank's fraud department or phishing reporting email.

  4. Report to the FTC

    File a report at reportfraud.ftc.gov.

  5. Report to the FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center

    File a complaint at ic3.gov for internet-related scams.

Identity Theft

  1. Place a fraud alert on your credit reports

    Contact one of the three major credit bureaus to place a fraud alert on your credit reports.

  2. Request credit reports

    Get free copies of your credit reports from annualcreditreport.com.

  3. Create an identity theft report

    Visit identitytheft.gov to report identity theft and get a recovery plan.

  4. Consider a credit freeze

    Contact each credit bureau to place a credit freeze on your credit reports.

  5. File a police report

    Contact your local police department to file a report about the identity theft.

  6. Update passwords and monitor accounts

    Change passwords for all your financial accounts and monitor them regularly for suspicious activity.
