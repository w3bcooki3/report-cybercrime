EMERGENCY RESPONSE

Immediate Steps to Take

If you believe you've been the victim of a phishing attack, follow these steps immediately

1

Disconnect From the Internet

If you suspect your device has been compromised, immediately disconnect from the internet by turning off Wi-Fi or unplugging your ethernet cable. This prevents malware from communicating with remote servers and limits potential damage.

2

Change Critical Passwords

From a different, secure device, immediately change passwords for important accounts like email, banking, and social media. Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication where available.

3

Alert Financial Institutions

If you shared financial information or suspect unauthorized access to your accounts, contact your bank, credit card companies, and other financial institutions immediately to freeze accounts or issue new cards.

4

Scan for Malware

Run a full system scan using reputable antivirus software. Consider using multiple scanning tools as some malware can evade detection by certain programs.

5

Report the Attack

Report the phishing attempt to relevant authorities and organizations like your IT department, the FTC, FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center, and the Anti-Phishing Working Group.
